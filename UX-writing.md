# UX Writing Portfolio - Fictional Examples (Google Style)

This is a **fictional UX Writing showcase**, designed according to **Google UX Writing style guidelines**.  
It demonstrates clear, concise, user-focused microcopy for digital products.

---

## 1. Onboarding / Welcome Screens
- **Welcome Message:**  
  *“Welcome to Medito! Let’s start your first meditation.”*  
- **Benefits Summary:**  
  *“Just 5 minutes a day can help you relax and focus better.”*  
- **CTA Buttons (action-oriented):**  
  - Primary: *“Get Started”*  
  - Secondary: *“Learn More”*

---

## 2. Forms & Inputs
- **Placeholder text:**  
  - Name: *“Your name”*  
  - Email: *“you@example.com”*  
- **Inline guidance:**  
  - Password: *“At least 8 characters and one number”*  
- **Success message:**  
  *“Profile updated successfully.”*  

*Google style tips applied:* short, clear, no jargon, active voice.

---

## 3. Empty States
- **No content yet:**  
  *“No sessions yet. Start your first meditation now.”*  
- **Search returns no results:**  
  *“We couldn’t find any matches. Try a different keyword.”*

*Principle:* tell users what’s happening and what to do next.

---

## 4. Error Messages
- **Form error:**  
  *“Enter a valid email address.”*  
- **Network error:**  
  *“Cannot connect. Check your internet and try again.”*  
- **Action blocked:**  
  *“Complete onboarding before using this feature.”*

*Principle:* neutral, helpful, concise, avoids blame.

---

## 5. Buttons / CTAs
- Primary (main action): *“Start now”*, *“Continue”*  
- Secondary (optional): *“Later”*, *“Back”*  
- Disabled (requires action): *“Complete previous step first”*

*Tip:* use verbs, be direct, consistent across screens.

---

## 6. Notifications / Feedback
- **Push notification:**  
  *“Time for your daily meditation. Let’s relax together.”*  
- **In-app notification:**  
  *“You’ve meditated 3 days in a row! Keep going.”*

*Principle:* friendly, motivating, concise.

---

## 💡 Notes
- All text is **fictional**, safe for portfolio use.  
- Follows **Google UX Writing principles**: clear, concise, user-focused, actionable, consistent.  
- Can be paired with **screenshots, prototypes, or Figma files** to demonstrate context.
